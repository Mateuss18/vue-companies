## A little bit about us
We are Belvo, an open finance API platform with the bold vision of democratizing access to financial services in Latin America. We enable any financial innovator to access and interpret financial data, as well as initiate payments from their end-users accounts.

We’re a highly-technical, passionate, and driven team. We are more than 90 people and our team currently represents 20 nationalities. We have offices in São Paulo and Mexico City – while a large portion of us work remotely.

We are tackling a very stimulating problem: connecting fintech innovators with legacy financial infrastructure. We strive to go beyond the limits of what is possible today and to do so in an elegant and developer-first way.

Since starting our adventure in May 2019, we have raised $71m from the leading VC investors globally.

## About the team

Our products are built for developers, by developers. Technological excellence is at the heart of what we do.

## Your opportunity
Our Open Finance team is building the unified infrastructure that powers both data aggregation and payment initiation in Brazil. You will design and develop services that retrieve and normalize complex financial information, while simultaneously building the robust, secure architecture required to move money through Pix and other Open Finance payment methods.

## Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain connections to financial institutions to retrieve data and initiate payments via Open Finance
- Leverage LLMs and AI tools to automate the normalization and enrichment of data into homogeneous endpoints that power our API
- Design and develop robust software architecture to support regulated payments environments and data retrieval at scale
- Adopt an AI-first mindset to accelerate development, from using coding assistants to building internal AI agents that improve system reliability
- Own the creation of programmatic tools, services, and workflows to improve the reliability of the Open Finance ecosystem
- Implement AI-powered monitoring and automated quality assurance to proactively detect anomalies in mission-critical financial services
- Interface with other institutions’ engineering teams or the Open Finance Service Desk to debug and resolve technical bottlenecks
- Carefully manage API retro-compatibility and deprecation plans for growing products
